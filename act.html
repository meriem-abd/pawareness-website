community involvement

if every store and cafe put clean water bowls near their stores

A simple bowl of clean water can make a world of difference for stray cats, especially in warmer months. Cafés and small shops can easily place bowls outside their doors, ensuring they’re regularly cleaned and refilled. This not only keeps cats hydrated but also builds a positive image of the business within the community. A small act like this shows that kindness can be part of everyday life.

Food Shops Turning Waste into Care

Many food shops discard scraps of meat or fish at the end of the day, but these leftovers can be repurposed to feed hungry cats. By placing safe, fresh portions in small plates outside, businesses reduce waste and contribute directly to animal welfare. Small shaded shelters give stray cats a safe, cool place to rest away from danger.

Caring for cats isn’t just about food and water—it’s also about safety and comfort. Shops can place simple resting shelters, such as covered boxes, insulated crates, or shaded benches, near their storefronts. These spots protect cats from extreme heat, rain, and street hazards. Even a single shaded shelter can make a huge difference, offering cats a sense of stability and security in busy urban spaces.
