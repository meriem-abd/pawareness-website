treating fleas

This page explains simple, low-cost ways to remove fleas from stray kittens. With just basic tools and a few minutes of care, you can help them feel comfortable, regain strength, and change their lives for the better.

⚠️Why Remove Fleas Quickly?

Fleas can drain a kitten’s blood, leading to anemia, weakness, and in severe cases, death. Removing them quickly brings relief and protects the kitten’s health.

materials

flea comb

dish soap or fleas shampoo

drying cloth

plastic basin

gloves

If the kitten is under 8 weeks old or has only a few fleas, comb its fur daily with a fine comb, dipping the comb in soapy water to trap fleas.

For kittens over 8 weeks old, fill a plastic basin with warm water and a small amount of dish soap to bathe them.

Wearing gloves, apply a little dish soap around their neck to prevent fleas from crawling to the head, eyes, and ears.

Bathe the cat in the soapy water, gently rubbing the fur. You may notice some blood spots from fleas — keep water away from the ears.

After rinsing thoroughly, dry the cat with a clean cloth.
