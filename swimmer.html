treating the Swimmer Syndrome

Swimmer Syndrome causes kittens’ legs to splay, making walking hard. Early help can restore movement and this page is here to show you how to help treating it.

⚠️ Why Treat Swimmer Syndrome Early?

If left untreated, Swimmer Syndrome can permanently affect a kitten’s ability to walk. Early care helps the legs grow stronger and gives the kitten a normal life.

materials

medical tape

Steps:

with the help of someone, gently hold the cat and guide its legs under the body.

use a medical tape or bandage to hold the legs in the correct position

Do short, gentle leg exercises daily

the cat is supposed to be starting to hold itself in 3-7 days if you see no improvement seek vet care
